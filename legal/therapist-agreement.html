Professional User Agreement

Last Updated: December 30, 2025 | Clinical Reviewer Program

1. Clinical Review & Quality Assurance

You are entering "Therapist Mode" as a Clinical Reviewer. Your role is to oversee, correct, and improve the AI's responses to users who need support.

The Workflow:

  • Users flag responses they find unhelpful or request additional support.
  • You receive a review ticket with relevant details.
  • You view an anonymized summary of the situation (to preserve user privacy) and the AI's response.
  • You provide an improved response using clinical best practices.
  • Your improved response is delivered to the user and helps train future AI models.

You assign to the Developer (Zahui Varga Erik) all intellectual property rights in your Contributions. This allows us to incorporate your clinical expertise into the App and share improvements with all users.

3. Privacy & Confidentiality

To protect user privacy, you receive only anonymized case summaries. You agree to:

  • Strict Confidentiality: You will not disclose specific case details outside the platform.
  • No Re-identification: You will not attempt to deduce the identity of the End User based on the summary details.
  • Data Hygiene: You will not screenshot, copy, or export the summaries to external devices.

4. Clinical Standard of Care

Your rewrites affect real users in real-time. You adhere to the following standard:

  • Safety First: Prioritize crisis intervention protocols (referring to 112/911) over therapeutic exploration when danger is present.
  • Evidence-Based: Interventions should align with CBT, DBT, or other recognized clinical frameworks.
  • Liability: You represent that your Contributions are clinically sound. You agree to indemnify the Developer against claims arising from gross negligence or malpractice in your corrections.
